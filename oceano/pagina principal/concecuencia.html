Consequences

Damage to Marine Ocosystems

The Plastic Island

Its nickname, the eighth continent, says it all, and is no exaggeration. Lying between California
and Hawaii, the Great Pacific Garbage Patch is three times the size of France and is the world's
biggest ocean waste repository, with 1.8 billion pieces of floating plastic which kill thousands of
marine animals each year.

Environment Degradation

Plastics release toxic chemicals that can be harmful to human health and the environment.
Some of these chemicals can last for over 400 years in the environment, further
exacerbating the problem of plastic pollution. It is important to reduce the use of plastics,
especially singleuse ones, and promote more sustainable alternatives to address this issue.
