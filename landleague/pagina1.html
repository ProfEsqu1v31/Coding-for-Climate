Why are agrochemicals used?


Farmer watering the field with agrochemicals and then reusing the land.

Agrochemical fertilizers are used to inject much-needed nutrients, such as nitrogen or potassium, directly into the soil to help plants grow. Different types of herbicides kill unnecessary weeds, allowing only the chosen plant to grow without competence for food, which can be used to grow larger fruits and vegetables. Another use is to prevent pests from ruining the crop. The reason why we use agrochemicals is mainly because the land loses its fertility due to the combination of polluting processes caused by human beings.
