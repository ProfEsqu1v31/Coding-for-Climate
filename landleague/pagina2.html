What are the consequences from its use?


Globally, the excessive use of nitrogen and phosphorus fertilizers can lead to the deterioration and eutrophication of surface water bodies, leading to the reduction of certain species of importance of aquatic systems. Other problems may include:


  • Soil bacteria are unable to develop.
  • Lack of flavor in fruits and vegetables.
  • Lack of humus due to the abuse of chemical fertilizers.
  • Propensity to pests and diseases.

The use of agrochemicals can cause long-term health problems for the workers who apply them into the land and for the people who live near the area, among the main health problems are sterility, aplastic anemia, cancer and various disorders. The children that a person may have who was exposed to the use of agrochemicals may also be affected, they can suffer teratogenesis, mutagenesis, alterations of the immune system of the central nervous system.
